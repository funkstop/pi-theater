Random points are scattered uniformly inside a square that perfectly encloses a quarter-circle of radius 1 — the ratio of points landing inside the circle to the total converges to π/4, since the circle's area is πr² and the square's area is 4r². Multiplying that ratio by 4 gives us our estimate of π, growing more accurate as the number of samples increases.
π ≈ 4 × inside / total
