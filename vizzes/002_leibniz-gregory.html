The Leibniz–Gregory formula states that π/4 equals the alternating sum of reciprocals of all odd integers, discovered independently by Leibniz and Gregory in the 17th century. Each additional term nudges the partial sum closer to π/4, so multiplying by 4 yields an ever-better estimate of π — though convergence is famously slow.
