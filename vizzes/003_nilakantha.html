The Nilakantha series (15th century, Kerala school) expresses π as 3 plus an alternating sum of fractions whose denominators are products of three consecutive even-rooted integers. Each additional term corrects the estimate, converging to π far faster than the Leibniz formula — just 10 terms already yield 6 correct decimal places.
